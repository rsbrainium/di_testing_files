## Exercise 1 — Persona value map

**Answer:**

| Persona | What they care about most | One-sentence value statement | Proof point (to verify) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CFO** | Cost of errors and risk during growth | "As you scale to a second site, we cut the cost of shipping errors and chargebacks so growth doesn't quietly erode margin." | Average error-cost reduction figure from a comparable customer |
| **VP of Operations** | Fewer fire drills, visibility across sites | "We give you one real-time view across both warehouses, so delays get caught before customers feel them." | Reduction in late-delivery rate at a similar two-site retailer |
| **Head of Supply Chain Ops (new hire)** | A quick, credible early win | "We're fast to deploy and give you a clean baseline of delivery performance in week one — an easy first win to show leadership." | Typical time-to-first-dashboard / onboarding timeline |

**Why it's correct:** The product never changes — the *outcome emphasized* changes per persona. The CFO answer leads with money and risk, the VP of Ops answer leads with control and prevention, and the new hire answer leads with a fast, visible win. Each proof point is marked "to verify," reinforcing that claims must be backed by real data before they reach a buyer.

## Exercise 2 — Prioritize the trigger events

**Answer (one strong example — scoring can vary if justified):**

| Signal | Relevance (1–5) | Freshness (1–5) | Rank |
|---|---|---|---|
| Second warehouse opened | 5 | 4 | **1** |
| Reported holiday delivery delays | 5 | 2 | 2 |
| Hiring Head of Supply Chain Ops | 4 | 5 | 3 |
| CEO "investing in customer experience" | 3 | 3 | 4 |

**Justification for the #1 choice:**

> The second-warehouse opening is both highly relevant and fresh: it directly creates the problem Northwind solves (visibility and error control across multiple sites), and it's recent enough that the pain is active right now. It points most naturally to the VP of Operations, who owns multi-site execution and is feeling the growth strain first.

**Why it's correct:** A defensible answer weighs *both* relevance (does it map to our product?) and freshness (is the window still open?). The delivery-delays signal is equally relevant but older, so its urgency has faded; the new-hire signal is fresh but the person may not be in seat yet. Reasoning matters more than the exact numbers — any ranking is acceptable if the justification holds together.
