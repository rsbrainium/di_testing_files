## Scenario

*Scenario (fictional):* You are a sales rep at **Northwind Logistics**, a company that sells freight-management software to mid-size retailers. The software reduces shipping errors and gives real-time visibility into deliveries.

Your target account is **Lumio Retail**, a fictional mid-size home-goods retailer. You've heard Lumio recently opened a second distribution warehouse to keep up with growth. Your job today is to research the account, find an angle, and draft an opening message — using AI as your research and drafting buddy, and verifying its output before you trust it.

> Because this is a learning scenario, treat any "facts" the AI returns about the fictional Lumio Retail as practice material. The skill you're building is the *process*: scaffold, research, verify, personalize.

## Exercise 1 — Scaffold a research prompt

**⏱️ Estimated time: 10 minutes**

A vague prompt gives vague research. Here you'll write a scaffolded prompt that tells the AI exactly who you are, what you sell, and what you need.

### Instructions

1. Open the Google Doc template and find the Exercise 1 answer box.
2. Write a research prompt for an AI assistant that includes all three scaffolding pillars:
   - **Context** — who you are (Northwind rep), what you sell, and the target (Lumio Retail, just opened a second warehouse).
   - **Instructions** — what you want back (e.g., recent news, likely challenges, possible decision-makers) and in what format (a short bulleted brief).
   - **Constraint** — one line telling the AI not to invent facts and to flag anything it isn't sure about.
3. Fill in the checklist table below to confirm your prompt contains each element.

|---|---|

| "Don't invent facts" constraint | |

**⏱️ Estimated time: 10 minutes**
