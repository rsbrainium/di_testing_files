## Scenario

*Scenario (fictional):* Northwind Logistics is scaling its sales team from 4 reps to 12. Leadership wants every new rep to research and open accounts the same high-quality way — fast, personalized, and *safe* (no made-up claims reaching buyers). You've been asked to design the **AI-assisted prospecting playbook** the whole team will follow on accounts like Lumio Retail.

There's no single right answer here. A good playbook balances speed against the real risk that AI surfaces wrong facts, and it has to be simple enough that a brand-new rep can follow it.

## Exercise 1 — Design the prospecting playbook

**⏱️ Estimated time: 45 minutes**

### Context

You're writing the document a new rep opens on day one. It should take an account from "name on a list" to "personalized first message" using AI at each step — with explicit checkpoints where the human verifies before trusting the AI.

### What You Will Practice

- Turning loose tactics into a repeatable, teachable process
- Designing verification guardrails
- Justifying where speed is fine and where it isn't

### Instructions

1. Define the workflow as **5–7 numbered steps**, from account research to a sent opener. For each step, specify: the goal, the AI prompt you'd use (scaffolded), and the human verification action before moving on.
2. Add a short **"Guardrails"** section: list at least three specific failure modes (e.g., AI invents a job title, cites an outdated funding figure) and the check that catches each one.
3. Add a **"Speed vs. accuracy"** note: name one step where it's fine to move fast, and one where verification is non-negotiable — and explain why.

### Research Pointers

You may want to look into how "trigger-based selling" and "signal-based prospecting" are described by sales teams, and how deep-research AI agents (like Microsoft 365 Copilot's Researcher) handle source citation — both are useful inputs for designing verification steps.

### Deliverable Format

A structured playbook document with: a numbered 5–7 step workflow (each with goal, prompt, verification), a Guardrails list (3+ failure modes + checks), and a short Speed vs. accuracy note.
