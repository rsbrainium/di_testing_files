## Exercise 1 — Find the themes, then sharpen the lens

### Part A — Analysis

**Answer:**

The data points to two dominant themes — **communication / late information** and **lack of recognition** — plus a secondary thread on **promotion visibility and workload planning.**

| Criterion | Analysis | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Top themes (all employees) | (1) Communication gaps — last-minute shift changes, hard-to-reach managers, patchy cross-team updates. (2) Recognition — "never hear when I've done well," night shifts unrecognised. (3) Promotion visibility / workload planning (weaker). | Treat communication and recognition as the two real priorities; the third is a watch-item. |
| How the picture changed under the constraint | Filtering to **Remote** employees pushes *communication* clearly to the top ("hard to reach my manager," "patchy between teams," "find out things late") while recognition stays present but secondary. The lowest scores (2s) cluster around communication. | The constraint shows communication is the more *acute* pain for remote staff, not just the most frequent overall. |
| Priority theme + why (cite the data) | **Communication.** It appears across every department, drives the lowest scores (the three 2-rated comments are all communication-related), and worsens for remote staff who can't compensate with hallway conversations. | Start with communication; it is both the most widespread and tied to the weakest scores. |

**Why this is correct:** the exercise rewards directing the AI rather than accepting its first pass. A student who only ran the first prompt would likely tie communication and recognition. Adding the *Remote* constraint is what reveals communication as the deeper problem — and the justification cites the score pattern, not a hunch.

## Exercise 2 — Three-month plan and a privacy check

### Part A — Three-month plan

**Answer:**

| Month | Focus | Specific actions (tailored to Brightline) |
|---|---|---|
| Month 1 | Diagnose & Communicate | Confirm the theme with a short pulse survey; managers commit to a fixed weekly update so shift and route changes never land last-minute; explain *why* changes happen. |
| Month 2 | Launch Initiatives | Async-friendly channels so remote Customer Care and night-shift Warehouse staff get the same information; a simple recognition ritual that works off-hours (e.g., logged shout-outs visible at shift start, not a 9am call). |
| Month 3 | Measure & Adjust | Re-run the engagement pulse; compare communication and recognition scores against the baseline; keep what moved the numbers, drop what didn't. |

**Why this is correct:** the plan ties directly back to the data (communication + recognition), and the tailoring fixes the obvious trap — recognition and updates that assume everyone is online during the day, which would exclude exactly the remote and night-shift staff who scored lowest.

### Part B — Privacy note (example)

**Answer:**

> *"The dataset is already anonymised — no names or IDs — and I kept every finding at the theme/department level so no single comment can be traced to a person. I would not paste raw, named exit-interview notes or individual performance scores into a general AI tool; that is PII and belongs only in a tool IT/legal has approved, handled under our GDPR/CCPA obligations."*

**Why this is correct:** it shows the two habits from the lesson — keep summaries broad enough that no individual is identifiable, and never put PII into an unapproved tool.
