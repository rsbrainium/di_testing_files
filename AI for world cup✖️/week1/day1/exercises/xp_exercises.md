## Scenario

**Brightline Logistics** *(fictional company)* is a 600-person regional logistics firm. The People team is hiring a **Data Analyst with 3–5 years of experience** to work on delivery and route data. A manager has handed you a rough job description to "tidy up" before it goes live:

> *"We're after an energetic, high-energy digital native who can hit the ground running in our fast-paced environment. Must be a recent graduate with a strong degree, a great culture fit, and the stamina to thrive under pressure. The ideal candidate is a young, dynamic self-starter."*

You will work with this same posting and role throughout the exercises. Use any AI assistant (Microsoft Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini).

## Exercise 1 — Spot and replace exclusionary wording

**⏱️ Estimated time: 10 minutes**

The draft above contains several phrases that could discourage qualified applicants without naming any protected characteristic.

### Instructions

## Exercise 2 — Rewrite the posting and build a rubric

**⏱️ Estimated time: 10 minutes**

Now turn the cleaned-up wording into a usable posting and the start of a fair interview.

### Instructions

1. Prompt the AI to *"Rewrite this job description for a Data Analyst (3–5 years) with an inclusive tone and skill-based qualifications,"* using the replacements from Exercise 1.
2. With one follow-up prompt, ask it to *"Create a 3-criteria hiring rubric scored 1–5 from this description, with a short description of weak / adequate / strong performance for each."*
3. Ask for *"one open-ended, behaviour-based interview question per criterion that avoids assumptions about background or personality."*
