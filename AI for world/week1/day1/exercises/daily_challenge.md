## Instructions

### Context

**Brightline Logistics** *(fictional company, 600 people)* wants to start using AI in HR but is nervous about getting it wrong. The Head of People has asked you for a one-page brief she can take to the leadership team. Three tasks are on the table:

- **Task A:** Draft and refine job descriptions for a range of open roles (each role is different).
- **Task B:** Answer the same recurring policy questions employees ask every week (leave, remote work, benefits).
- **Task C:** Analyse the anonymised annual engagement survey to find improvement themes.

You have everything you need below — no outside research required.

<br>

---

### Your Mission

Recommend where Brightline should start, decide whether each task needs a prompt or an agent, and set the guardrails that keep the rollout fair and safe.

<br>

---

### Part A — Prompt or agent?

1. For each task (A, B, C), decide whether it is better served by a **one-off prompt** or a **persistent agent**, and give a one-sentence reason tied to how often it repeats and how much consistency matters.
2. Identify which task is the safest place to **start small** and explain why (lowest risk, fast visible win).

<br>

---

### Part B — Design the riskiest piece

1. The recurring policy questions (Task B) are the candidate for an agent. Write its **one-sentence purpose**, its **grounding source**, and **two boundaries** where it must redirect to a human.
2. Write the **escalation message** an employee would see when the agent hits a boundary.

<br>

---

### Part C — Guardrails

1. Write three short guardrails covering **bias**, **privacy/PII**, and **human accountability** — one sentence each, phrased so a non-technical leader understands them.
2. Add one sentence naming a situation at Brightline where AI is NOT the right tool.

<br>

---

## Deliverable

A single one-page brief containing: the prompt-vs-agent decision for Tasks A–C, your "start here" recommendation, the policy-agent mini-spec from Part B (purpose, grounding, two boundaries, escalation message), and the three guardrails plus the "not the right tool" note from Part C.
