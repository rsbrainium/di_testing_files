## Scenario

**Brightline Logistics** *(fictional company)* ran its annual engagement survey. You have an **anonymised** export with these columns: `Department`, `Location` (Remote / On-site), `Engagement_Score` (1–5), and a free-text `Comments` field. There are no names or IDs. Leadership wants to know what to act on this quarter. Below is a representative sample of the comments and scores — treat this as the dataset you feed your AI tool.

| Department | Location | Score | Comment (sample) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Operations | On-site | 2 | "Shift changes get announced last minute. No one tells us why." |
| Operations | On-site | 3 | "Good team, but I never hear when I've done well." |
| Customer Care | Remote | 2 | "Hard to reach my manager. Updates come too late to act on." |
| Customer Care | Remote | 4 | "Flexibility is great. Recognition is rare though." |
| Finance | On-site | 3 | "Workload is heavy at month-end; planning could be clearer." |
| Tech | Remote | 4 | "Clear goals. Would like more visibility into promotions." |
| Tech | Remote | 2 | "Communication between teams is patchy. I find out things late." |
| Warehouse | On-site | 3 | "Recognition almost never happens for night shifts." |

## Exercise 1 — Find the themes, then sharpen the lens

**⏱️ Estimated time: 15 minutes**

### Context

Two themes appear again and again in this data, but the strength of each shifts depending on which group you look at. Your job is to direct the AI precisely, not just accept its first summary.

### What You Will Practice
- Iterative prompting with constraints
- Distinguishing collective themes from individual comments

### Instructions

1. Give the AI the table above and prompt: *"Identify the top three improvement themes across this engagement data and the approximate score impact of each."*
2. Re-prompt with **one constraint** that changes the picture — for example, *"Now focus only on Remote employees"* or *"Exclude any comment that is mostly positive."* Note how the ranking shifts.
3. Decide which **one** theme Brightline should tackle first, and justify the choice in 2–3 sentences using the data (not a hunch).

### Deliverable Format

| Criterion | Analysis | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Top themes (all employees) | | |
| How the picture changed under your constraint | | |
| Priority theme + why (cite the data) | | |

## Exercise 2 — Three-month plan and a privacy check

**⏱️ Estimated time: 15 minutes**

### Context

A theme is only useful once it becomes action. You will turn your priority theme into a plan leadership can run — and confirm you handled the data responsibly.

### What You Will Practice
- Turning insight into a structured action plan
- Applying privacy judgment to an AI-assisted analysis

### Instructions

1. Prompt the AI: *"Suggest engagement actions for [your priority theme], structured as a three-month plan: Month 1 Diagnose & Communicate, Month 2 Launch Initiatives, Month 3 Measure & Adjust."*
2. Refine at least one suggestion so it fits a 600-person logistics firm (for example, night-shift recognition that does not depend on everyone being online at 9am).
3. Write a 2–3 sentence privacy note: what you did to avoid singling out any individual, and one thing you would NOT paste into an unapproved AI tool.
